• Utah Trauma System Advisory Committee

• Guidelines for transport of Trauma Patients to Freestanding Emergency Departments

• The following types of patients are NOT candidates for transport to a freestanding ED (FSED):

• Critically-injured patients with unstable vital signs or other life-threatening conditions UNLESS the patient’s airway is not maintainable with EMS advanced or basic airway management techniques and the FSED is the closest ED

• Traumatic cardiac arrest patients

• Patients meeting Steps 1-3 criteria of the Utah Trauma Field Triage Guidelines

• Patients with head injuries who are over 65 years old OR who are taking anticoagulants

• Patients with angulated long bone fractures

• Patients with suspected open fractures or dislocations

• EMS provider judgement

• THESE GUIDELINES MAY BE MODIFIED DURING A DISASTER SITUATION
